Social Media Checklist for College Students
Responsible Use — Do's and Don'ts

 Do's

| 1 | Think before you post — Ask yourself: "Would I be okay if my professor, future employer, or family saw this?" 
| 2 | Keep your profiles professional — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a consistent username across platforms. 
| 3 | Use privacy settings — Regularly review and update who can see your posts, photos, and personal information. 
| 4 | Give credit where it's due — Always tag, mention, or cite the original creator when sharing someone else's content. 
| 5 | Be mindful of your digital footprint — Remember that what you post today can be found years from now by employers and institutions. 
| 6 | Engage respectfully in discussions — Share your opinions constructively and stay open to different perspectives. 
| 7 | Take regular breaks — Set screen time limits to protect your mental health and academic focus. 



Don'ts

| # | Don't |
|---|--------|
| 1 | Don't share personal information publicly — Avoid posting your phone number, address, class schedule, or location in real time. 
| 2 | Don't post or share content in anger — Heated posts are hard to take back and can damage relationships and reputation. 
| 3 | Don't plagiarize or spread misinformation — Always verify information before sharing and never pass off others' work as your own. 
| 4 | Don't overshare about others — Avoid posting photos, stories, or details about friends without their consent. 
| 5 | Don't neglect academics for social media — Avoid letting scrolling habits eat into study time, sleep, or deadlines. 
| 6 | Don't engage with cyberbullying — Neither participate in nor ignore it — report and block where necessary. 
| 7 | Don't assume private messages are truly private — Screenshots exist. Treat every message as potentially shareable.
