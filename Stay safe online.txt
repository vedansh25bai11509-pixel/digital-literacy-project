

Passwords and Accounts

Don't keep your password as your name, birthday, or "123456". Use something random with numbers and symbols. Yes it's annoying but it matters.
Turn on 2-step verification (2FA) on your Gmail, Instagram, and banking apps. It takes 2 minutes and saves a lot of trouble later.
Never ever share your OTP with anyone. Not even someone who says they are from your bank or from Google. Real companies never ask for OTP on call.

UPI and Money Safety (Very Important)

If someone sends you a QR code and says "scan this to receive money" — that is 100% a scam. QR codes are only for sending money, not receiving. This one scam alone has fooled thousands of students.
Before sending money on PhonePe, GPay or any UPI app — check the name of the person properly. Scammers make fake IDs that look almost real. If someone is rushing you to pay fast, that itself is a red flag.

Your Phone and Apps

Only download apps from Play Store or App Store. If someone sends you a link to download an app from WhatsApp or a website, just don't do it.
Update your phone software when it asks you to. Those updates fix security problems. Ignoring them for months is not great.
Put a lock on your phone. If it gets lost, at least your data won't be easily accessed by someone else.

While Using Internet

Don't use college canteen WiFi or any public WiFi to do banking or UPI transfers. Anyone on the same network can try to steal your data. Use your mobile data for that.
Don't click random links that come on WhatsApp saying "you won a prize" or "your account will be blocked". These are fake. Just delete them.
Don't put your phone number or home address publicly on Instagram or any social media. You don't know who is watching.

Things Specific to Students

If you see a post saying "work from home, earn 500 rupees per hour, just pay 200 registration fee first" — it's a scam. Legit jobs never ask you to pay first.
Buying second-hand stuff on OLX or Facebook? Never pay in advance to a stranger. Meet in person or use a trusted platform.


If Something Goes Wrong – Report Here

Don't feel embarrassed if you got scammed. Just report it fast, the faster you report the better chance you have of getting money back.

| Where to Report | Details |
|National Cyber Crime Website | cybercrime.gov.in |
|Helpline Number | Call 1930 (works 24/7) |
|local Police | Go to nearest police station and file a complaint |

Calling 1930 quickly after a fraud can actually help freeze the transaction before the money is gone. So don't wait.
